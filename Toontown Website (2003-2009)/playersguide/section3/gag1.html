Use your gags to battle the Cogs and make them fall apart with laughter! There are seven types of gags: Toon-Up, Trap, Lure, Sound, Throw, Squirt, and Drop.
Use the jellybeans you’ve earned playing Trolley games to buy gags at one of the Goofy’s Gag Shop locations in any of Toontown’s playgrounds.

Some ToonTasks allow you to carry more jellybeans. These are important tasks because the more jellybeans you carry the more often you’ll be able to go to the gag shop and restock quickly. Similarly, some ToonTasks allow you to carry more gags. The more gags you carry the less often you’ll need to go to the gag shop and restock before heading back into battle.
As you advance in the game by completing ToonTasks and increasing your gag skills in battles, you’ll earn new and increasingly powerful gags.

You start Toontown with the Squirting Flower and Cupcake gags, the first gags in the Squirt and Throw tracks. By battling Cogs, you’ll earn skill points for using those gags. You can see the amount of skill you’ll earn for using that gag in your battle menu and on the gags page of your Shticker book.

If the gag you’re using is too powerful for the Cogs you’re battling, you won’t get any skill credit for using it. You can see this information on the right side of the battle menu display when you roll over any of your gags. Or simply chose from the gags with the blue background in the battle menu.
 
To make sure you always get skill credit for your gags, follow these basic rules.
 
Gag level 1 (example: Squirting Flower) Use on level 1 and higher Cogs
Gag level 2 (example: Glass of Water) Use on level 2 and higher Cogs
Gag level 3 (example: Squirt Gun) Use on level 3 and higher Cogs
Gag level 4 (example: Seltzer Bottle) Use on level 4 and higher Cogs
Gag level 5 (example: Fire Hose) Use on level 5 and higher Cogs
Gag level 6 (example: Storm Cloud) Use on level 6 and higher Cogs
 
Toon Tip: Work together using gags from the same track on one Cog to get a damage bonus.
